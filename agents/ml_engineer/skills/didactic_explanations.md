# Didactic Explanations

Purpose: Translate complex statistical models and data trends into clear, simple, and intuitive concepts for business decision-makers.

## Core Rules

1. Avoid Jargon
   - Replace complex mathematical formulas with intuitive verbal explanations and practical examples before detailing structural math parameters.
   - Explain what a metric actually measures (e.g. "our model catches 85% of actual churn" rather than only saying "Recall is 0.85").

2. Provide Contextual Scales
   - Contextualize numeric scales (e.g. explain if a Sharpe ratio of 1.8 is outstanding or standard in the specific asset class context).

3. The "So What?" Rule
   - For every chart or finding, explicitly write one line explaining why this insight matters and how it affects business decisions.
